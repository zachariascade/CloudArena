# Slay the Spire 2 Strategy Cheat Sheet

## Core Idea

Slay the Spire 2 builds on the original, but adds:

- More **enemy disruption and counterplay**
- More **distinct character mechanics**
- More pressure to build **coherent engines early**

Success comes from building a **resilient engine**, not just a strong one.

---

## Resource Engines (More Defined & Character-Based)

### Energy Manipulation

- Still core to most builds
- More tied to relics + character systems
- Style: enable combo turns or sustain

### Card Flow (Draw + Selection)

- Stronger emphasis on _controlled draw_ (not just more cards)
- Choosing the right cards matters more than volume
- Style: consistency + precision

### Unique Resource Systems

- Some characters introduce new mechanics:
  - Necrobinder → companion-based actions
  - Regent → “stars” resource for powerful plays
- Style: custom mini-resource economy

---

## Damage Strategies (More Punishable if One-Dimensional)

### Scaling Damage

- Strength / Focus still key
- But slower builds are more punishable
- Style: must balance with defense

### Multi-Hit / Combo Damage

- Synergizes with buffs
- Vulnerable to disruption (enemy counters)
- Style: burst turns

### Damage Over Time

- Poison-style effects still exist
- More valuable due to enemy defenses
- Style: inevitability

---

## Defense Strategies (More Important Than Before)

### Block Engine

- Still core, but enemies punish passive play more
- Needs synergy, not just raw block cards
- Style: active defense

### Passive Defense Systems

- Frost / companions / effects that reduce damage
- Style: sustain over time

### Timing-Based Defense

- Intangible / mitigation effects
- Critical due to stronger enemy turns
- Style: precision defense

---

## Synergy / Combo Engines (More Fragile)

### Infinite / Near-Infinite Combos

- Still possible
- More likely to be countered by enemies
- Style: high-risk, high-reward

### Power Stacking

- Slower scaling builds
- Must survive longer to pay off
- Style: delayed payoff

### Engine-Based Decks

- Build around 1–2 core interactions
- More important than “good stuff” decks
- Style: focused synergy

---

## Deck Manipulation (More Important Than STS1)

### Thin Decks

- Even more valuable due to enemy disruption
- Consistency is king

### Card Selection / Filtering

- Choosing what _not_ to draw matters
- Style: precision deck control

---

## Enemy Interaction (BIG DIFFERENCE IN STS2)

### Counterplay Enemies

- Some enemies:
  - Exhaust your cards
  - Prevent drawing
  - Increase costs
- Style: your engine must survive disruption

### Anti-Strategy Bosses

- Certain bosses hard-counter specific builds
- Forces adaptability mid-run

## Subgame Mechanics (What Defines STS2)

### Engine vs Disruption

- Can your deck function when disrupted?

### Consistency vs Power

- Reliable decks outperform greedy ones more often

### Adaptation

- Builds must evolve mid-run

---

## Key Differences from STS1

- More enemy mechanics that **interfere with your deck**
- More emphasis on **tight, focused builds**
- New **character-specific systems**
- Higher punishment for **greedy or narrow strategies**

---

## Recommended Strategies to Explore

- Engine-based decks → core STS2 skill
- Flexible builds → survive counters
- Card filtering → consistency wins runs
- Balanced scaling → offense + defense together
- Disruption-resistant combos → next-level play
