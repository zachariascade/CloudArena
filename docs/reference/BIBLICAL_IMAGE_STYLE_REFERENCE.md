# Biblical Image Style Reference

Use this document when sourcing art for Biblical cards in Cloud Arcanum.

## Goal

Biblical card art should feel:

- mythic
- severe
- archetypal
- sacred
- premodern

It should read more like inherited visual tradition than modern entertainment concept art.

## Preferred Visual Sources

Prefer:

- engravings
- etchings
- woodcuts
- classical paintings
- manuscript illuminations
- museum-held religious art

## Preferred Tone

Look for images that feel:

- solemn rather than sentimental
- symbolically charged rather than literalistic
- weighty rather than flashy
- ancient rather than contemporary
- reverent without becoming soft or decorative

## Preferred Composition

Prefer compositions that:

- have one dominant focal subject
- crop cleanly into a card frame
- remain readable at small size
- avoid cluttered backgrounds
- preserve a strong silhouette

## Preferred Mood By Card Kind

Major figures:

- iconic
- dignified
- archetypal

Events:

- dramatic
- world-shaping
- high-contrast

Objects:

- symbolic
- isolated or compositionally clear

Locations:

- atmospheric
- monumental
- mythic in scale

## Avoid

Avoid:

- modern church graphics
- glossy digital fantasy illustration unless explicitly requested
- cartoon or anime treatments
- meme images
- low-resolution reposts
- heavily watermarked files
- overly busy compositions that will collapse at card size
- stock-photo realism

## Rights Guidance

Preferred rights order:

1. public domain museum download
2. Wikimedia Commons public-domain file
3. clearly reusable licensed file

If rights are unclear, do not use the image automatically.
