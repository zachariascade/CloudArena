# Biblical Ascension Deckbuilder

## Core Pitch

A biblical-themed roguelike deckbuilder inspired by *Slay the Spire* and *Magic: The Gathering*.

The player ascends a tower or mountain toward a heavenly destination while demonic forces try to stop that ascent. Rather than focusing on the attacks of a single hero, the player's power comes from biblical characters, relics, and spiritual effects represented in the deck.

## Core Fantasy

- Climb toward heaven through a series of battles and choices
- Build a deck during the run from humble beginnings
- Call on biblical figures who pray or intercede on your behalf
- Push back demonic opposition through intercessors, tactical card play, and synergistic board states
- Support solo play or cooperative play

## Setting And Structure

- The journey is an ascent up a sacred tower, mountain, or similar holy path
- Each run starts with a simple, basic deck
- As the player progresses upward, they gain new cards, relics, and upgrades
- Enemies are demonic, corrupting, deceptive, or obstructive forces trying to block ascension

## Gameplay Inspiration

### Slay the Spire Influence

- Run-based progression
- Start simple and build strength over time
- Climb through sequential encounters
- Draft or earn new tools as the run continues

### Magic: The Gathering Influence

- Board-building strategies
- Deck archetypes and synergies
- Graveyard and resurrection interactions
- Wide versus tall strategies
- Characters, relics, and spiritual effects functioning like card types or engines

## Tentative Combat Idea

The player may represent themselves, or possibly a central character to be defined later. The key difference from *Slay the Spire* is that the deck is not mainly a list of direct attack actions by one fighter.

Instead, the deck contains biblical characters who are praying for, supporting, or interceding on behalf of the player. Those characters function as active permanents on the battlefield, more like allied party members than passive stat pieces. They can attack, defend, support one another, and be defeated by enemy pressure over the course of a fight.

The current mechanical direction also assumes:
- *Slay the Spire*-style energy instead of mana buildup
- a fresh hand of cards each turn
- a persistent battlefield where intercessors, relics, and blessings carry the longer-term state of the fight

Spiritualized systems such as "prayer power" may still exist, but they are no longer assumed to be required for the core combat model to work.

Examples:
- Abraham could provide a style of prayer support or faith-based scaling
- Jesus could provide uniquely powerful intercession, protection, or transformation effects
- Relics could amplify prayer, resurrection, endurance, or divine favor

## Strategic Directions

- Go wide: many characters praying for you at once
- Go tall: one heavily enhanced intercessor or prayer engine
- Graveyard recursion: resurrect characters back onto the field
- Synergy builds: combine characters, relics, and effects into spiritual engines
- Incremental deck growth throughout the climb

## Cooperative Angle

The game can be cooperative. Possible directions:
- Multiple players climb together against shared demonic encounters
- Players build complementary prayer/intercession strategies
- Teammates support each other's board states, resurrection plans, or relic synergies

## Open Questions

- Is the player an unnamed pilgrim, a customizable avatar, or a defined protagonist?
- How literal should the biblical representation be for major figures like Jesus?
- Should the ascent be framed more as a mountain, a tower, a ladder, or multiple heavenly realms?
- How should cooperative turns and shared resources work?
- How much of the spiritual framing should live in explicit mechanics versus names, art, and card identity?

## Early Design Summary

This concept is a cooperative biblical roguelike deckbuilder where players ascend toward heaven while building a deck of biblical characters, relics, and spiritual effects. The main hook is replacing the usual single-character attack model with intercessors who take the field on your behalf, creating a sacred battlefield that grows over the course of a run.
