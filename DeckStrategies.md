# MTG Commander Strategy Cheat Sheet

## Core Idea

Commander decks aren’t just aggro/control/combo—they often revolve around a **mechanical identity** (a subgame or engine).

---

## Resource Engines

### Aristocrats (Sacrifice)

- Sacrifice creatures for value
- Payoffs: life drain, draw, tokens
- Style: grindy, resource conversion

### Blink (ETB Abuse)

- Exile + return creatures to retrigger abilities
- Style: value loops, timing decisions

### Reanimator

- Cheat big creatures from graveyard into play
- Style: explosive, high-impact turns

---

## Combat Strategies

### Tokens (Go-Wide)

- Many small creatures + buffs
- Strength: overwhelming numbers
- Weakness: board wipes

### Voltron

- One creature stacked with buffs/equipment
- Win via commander damage
- Style: high risk/reward

### Pillowfort (Defense/Tax)

- Discourage attacks with taxes/effects
- Style: stall and outlast

---

## Synergy Mechanics

### Storm

- Cast many spells in one turn
- Big payoff turn (“combo explosion”)

### +1/+1 Counters

- Creatures grow over time
- Style: scaling / snowball

### Spellslinger

- Instants/sorceries trigger effects
- Style: reactive, burst turns

---

## Board / System Mechanics

### Landfall

- Playing lands triggers effects
- Style: ramp + scaling value

### Graveyard Focus

- Treat graveyard like a second hand
- Style: recursion and looping

### Chaos

- Randomized effects and outcomes
- Style: unpredictable, casual

---

## Your Decks

### Final Fantasy X

- Mechanic: Encounters moving around
- Similar to: board manipulation / counters systems

### Final Fantasy VII

- Mechanic: Equipment + card value
- Similar to: Voltron + artifact synergy

---

## Recommended to Explore

- Aristocrats → resource engine
- Blink → ability looping
- Storm → “limit break” turns
- Counters → RPG-style progression
- Reanimator → cheat big threats
